Our Mission

Generate Health mobilizes and inspires the St. Louis region to advance racial equity in pregnancy outcomes, family well-being, and community health.


Focusing on Critical Priorities

  • Illuminating the root causes of racial disparities in infant mortality and maternal health
  • Advocating for the redirection of resources to eliminate racial disparities in infant mortality and maternal health
  • Catalyzing action within the ecosystem
  • Advance regional accountability for equitable systems


Accelerating Change By

  • Understanding our community’s needs – We help uncover the full picture of what impacts Black maternal and infant health to ensure efforts and investments in our region can have the greatest impact. We review local and national data and listen to families in our community to understand the factors that contribute to family health.

  • Educating service providers and community leaders – Working with our partners and members, we provide professional development, training, and education courses to further one’s understanding in the field of maternal and child health. Our seminars and trainings help service providers and regional planners better focus, align, and plan their work related to moms and babies.

  • Advocating for positive policy change – We advocate for improvements in community conditions and new policies to support families. We represent our membership of more than 300 organizations and 800 individuals to provide a strong collective voice for policy change at both the legislative- and service provider-levels in St. Louis.

  • Mobilizing the region around our most pressing maternal and child issues – We bring together the right resources to accelerate progress, including getting people to the table who don’t typically work together.
